IT autobiography

My IT life

I am Bryce and I am studying IT-management at Immaculata Instituut. I am 18 years old and I am the Apple man of the class. At home I mostly work on a Mac computer.

One of my first experiences with IT was when I was 6 years old. I had an old pc on which I could play some games. One day I got a new game from my parents, but they forgot to install it on the computer. So I tried to install it by myself. After a while I succeeded! I was very proud of myself. After that my fascination for computers became bigger and bigger. I started looking for some pictures on Google. shortly after that I found my way on the internet.

 

At this moment IT means a lot to me! Every day I browse the web, searching for some information for school, checking my social media accounts, and a lot more! I also achieved a few things, like playing games on a very high level, making websites (also a site for school) and building my own computer. There is a big evolution! Once I played games on a normal level, just for fun, but with some training and a lot of hours behind the screen I became very good at some games, like Call of Duty and some other race games. Of course I don’t see myself as an expert, because there are a lot of better and smarter people than me.

 

In the future I want to be a programmer in a big company, of course it will be very difficult to reach that goal, but I will work for it.  One of my ambitions is to make good mobile apps and earn a bit of money with it. Also working on projects seems very interesting to me. A big aspiration of me is to work as a project leader. In this function I can design my own stuff, and I have a team who will work with me to build that specific digital dream.

 

So my computer has become a very important part of me. Everything I do is mostly on a computer. Also to write things at school I use a tablet because my handwriting is very difficult to read. Also if I see a girl at school or somewhere else, I look her up on Facebook so I can talk to her. That’s much simpler than walking to her in real life! Also on Facebook I decide what everyone can see, in real life they see my full body and there are no ways to hide some parts, which is possible on Facebook.
